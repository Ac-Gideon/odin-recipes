Banku

Description

In Ghanaian cusine, banku is a swallow dish made of a slightly fermented cooked mixture of corn and cassava doughs formed into single-servings balls.Banku is cooked in hot water until it turns into a smooth,whitish paste,served with okra stew,soup or a pepper sauce with fish.

Ingredients

  • Corn dough
  • Cassava dough
  • Salt
  • Water

Preparation

  1. Thoroughly wash your hands before you begin to prepare the meal.
  2. Mix the corn dough with the cassava dough in a pot. Mix and mash the dough to form a smooth paste as you ensure that you remove all the fibre pieces and the lumps.
  3. Using your hands, continue mixing until the dough form a paste-like mixture. When it gains an extremely smooth consistency, add a pinch of salt.
  4. Place the pot with mixture on medium heat fire and stir gently using a wooden stirrer(Bankuta). Continue stirring gently until the paste begins to thicken.
  5. The mixture will continue to get thicker and thicker with time such that it begins to gather at bottom of the wooden stirrer. At this point, you will be required to add more force as it is getting harder.
  6. Lower the heat and using sackcloth or a napkin, hold the pot in one place and use a wooden stirrer to beat through the mixture. Continue with the beating for roughly six minutes as you make brief stops in between the beating.
  7. Create some perforations on the thick mass using the wooden stirrer and add water. Increase the fire, cover the pot and leave it for around 10 to 15 minutes to cook thoroughly.
  8. Check on the mixture to make sure that the water does not dry up completely. Repeat the process of kneading until you achieve the smoothness of your choice.
  9. When it gains the smoothness you prefer, take a smaller bowl and use it to shape it into you desired serving shapes and sizes.
  10. Enjoy with your preferred fish,sauce or soup.

Braised Rice

Description

Braised rice is a Ghanaian style of cooking rice. It is known as "anwa moo" in the Akan language,literally "oil rice". It is prepared with few ingredients, and it is usually balanced with some vegetables and any other accompaniment to balance the diet.

Ingredients

  • 3 Cups of rice
  • 1/2 Cup of diced carrot
  • Handful parsley
  • 1/4 Green peas
  • 2 Finely diced pepper
  • 1 Medium onion(diced)
  • Salt and seasoning
  • 3 Tablespoon Oil
  • 1/2 Tablespoon ginger and garlic paste
  • Spring onions

Preparation

  1. Heat up on medium flame. Sauté onion, then ginger and garlic paste until fragrant.
  2. Add finely diced pepper and sauté for another minute.
  3. Add water enough to cook the rice and bring to a rolling boil. Add washed rice and peas (if using fresh peas).
  4. Add salt and seasoning to taste. Stir and cover with a lid. Cook rice until tender.
  5. Add carrot and parsley at the last two minutes of cooking the rice.
  6. Fluff the rice with a fork and garnish with spring onion. Serve with stew or sauce of choice.
